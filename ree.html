Renewable Energy Engineering

What are Renewable Energy Sources?

Renewable energy sources are natural sources of energy that can be used again and again, such as sunlight, wind, water, and biomass. They are clean, sustainable, and reduce dependency on fossil fuels.

Types of Renewable Energy Sources in Agriculture

  • Solar Energy – Used for solar pumps, solar dryers, solar lights, and solar-powered irrigation systems.
  • Wind Energy – Small wind turbines can pump water or generate electricity for farms.
  • Biogas Energy – Produced from animal dung and crop waste; used for cooking, heating, and generating power.
  • Biomass Energy – Crop residues, wood, and other organic matter used for fuel and power generation.
  • Hydropower (Small-scale) – Small water turbines used for electricity and irrigation in areas with flowing water.

Uses for Farmers

  • Provides low-cost and sustainable energy for irrigation and farm operations.
  • Reduces fuel and electricity bills.
  • Helps in processing and storage through solar dryers, coolers, and biogas systems.
  • Improves rural living by providing clean cooking fuel and lighting.
  • Makes farming eco-friendly by reducing pollution and greenhouse gases.
  • Ensures energy availability even in remote areas where electricity is not reliable.
